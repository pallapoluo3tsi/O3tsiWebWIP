      A Global Investment Bank

      The bank provides global counterparty credit analysis and monitoring and identified that their credit reporting infrastructure needed to be expanded and enhanced to support the business growth, multiple geographical locations and evolving regulatory requirements; They required an assessment of their entire credit monitoring approach with an eye towards enhancement and increased functionality.

      Requirements
      • Assist with requirements gathering, vetting and framing a Global Counterparty Credit Reporting infrastructure including the meta-data components and delivery methods
      • Document the detailed credit reporting requirements for the technology build team in line with the firm’s evolution plans
      Approach
      • The process included understanding and documenting the current reporting processes, data sources, business logic, transformation rules, automated and manual processes that produced the current daily counterparty credit report suite
      • A meta-data dictionary was defined and organized into a representative normalized extraction that would support the evolution of current and future credit reports
      Contribution
      • Senior experienced market and credit risk practitioners provided an unbiased and deep knowledge in the domain and defined a detailed data structure based on industry best practice
      Results
      • The global credit reporting requirements were presented to the senior management and delivered to the global data team to frame and produce the extraction layer for the credit reports

      International Investment Bank

      Required consolidation of FX business under unified corporate treasury operations

      Requirements
      • Assist with requirements gathering, vetting and presenting a business vision and roadmap for formalizing global FX services within the treasury organization
      Approach

      • Analysis and requirements gathering was accomplished through a series of detailed interview sessions with the key stakeholders and management including and representatives from each of the firm’s global business line

      • The data was compiled into an executive FX Vision document that defined their current state, future state and transition roadmap, highlighting potential next steps and priorities in line with the firms’ evolution.
      Contribution
      • Senior experienced treasury, liquidity and cash management practitioners with extensive knowledge of FX transactions and operations provided the vision document
      Results
      • The executive summary analysis was presented to the Treasurer and management team to align with firm-wide priorities and dependencies supporting the business going forward in a phase approach
